> **Claude's Response:**
>
> **Organized by Action, not Date**
> Each section starts with an **action label** (`Plan`, `Build`, later maybe `Debug`, `Refactor`) followed by what that action targeted. Dates are gone from the structure entirely — if you need to know _when_, the scratchpad reference links give you that. This way when you scan the Dev Log, you see a story of **"what was done"** not "what happened on which day."
>
> **Small Decisions → Just Don't Include Them**
> This was your biggest friction point. Things like "date vs problem-name for filenames" — the answer is: **they don't appear here at all.** They live in the scratchpad where they were born. The Dev Log only shows the _outcome_ ("Date as filename, append throughout the day"). If you abandoned a decision and never came back to it, it simply doesn't exist in the Dev Log. No crossing out, no tracking. It was never important enough to leave the scratchpad.
>
> **`↳ Reference:` Links**
> Instead of duplicating reasoning, each section can link back to the scratchpad entries or task pages where the messy deliberation happened. The Dev Log stays concise; the depth is one click away.
>
> **Action Types to Define**
> Based on your scratchpad, I see these naturally emerging:
> - **Plan** — designing structure, making decisions about approach
> - **Build** — actually implementing something
> - **Debug** — fixing something broken (you mentioned this would be important)
> - **Review** — periodic look-back to reorganize or reassess
>
> You don't need to finalize these now — just use whatever verb feels right when you write a new section. The pattern will stabilize on its own.
