**Claude's Response:**

You're actually describing two different things that both matter — and the good news is they don't have to live in the same place.

**This Main Thread = your messy, honest diary.** It's where you dump thoughts as they come. Yes it's noisy, but that noise is valuable — it captures *why* you made decisions, what you were feeling, what confused you. That's exactly the "experience and memory" you said you wanted to preserve in your Goal.md. Future-you will thank present-you for writing "I calmed down and waited for a moment" — that's a real decision-making insight you'd lose with pure structure.

**Structured decision notes = separate files you create *when* a decision is clear enough to summarize.** Not every thought deserves its own structured note — only the ones that become actual decisions or turning points. You'd link to them from here, like you already did in the Obsidian Learning System with `[[Claude Desktop vs Claude Code Decision]]`.

So the system could be:
1. **Main Thread** — diary-style, dated entries, write freely, include the "noise"
2. **Decision notes** — created only when you reach a clear decision point, linked from Main Thread
3. Main Thread naturally becomes your index/timeline, decision notes become your clean reference

This way the messy thoughts aren't lost (they stay in Main Thread), but they also don't clutter your decision records. You get both honesty and navigability.

The key: don't create a decision note *before* you've actually decided. Write messily first, then extract structure when it emerges. That's lower friction than trying to be organized from the start.
